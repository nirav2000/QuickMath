    Fast Square Root Method (Step-by-Step)

    Step 1: Learn anchor squares

    Memorize 1² to 40² in chunks: 1-10, 11-20, 21-30, 31-40. Ex: 17²=289, 26²=676.

    Step 2: Bracket the number

    For √850, find nearby squares: 29²=841 and 30²=900, so answer is between 29 and 30.

    Step 3: Estimate proportionally

    850 is 9 above 841 out of gap 59, so √850 ≈ 29 + 9/59 ≈ 29.15.

    Step 4: Last-digit clues

    Perfect squares can end only in 0,1,4,5,6,9. Eg if number ends in 9, root often ends in 3 or 7 (for perfect squares).

    Step 5: Speed drills

    Do batches of 20. First focus on correctness, then reduce average time below 1500ms.

    Step 6: Error review

    Wrong answers are repeated sooner by spaced repetition so weak spots are reinforced.
